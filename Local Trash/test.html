Mesoamerican Voices:
Chapter 1:
- “Mesoamerica” = geographical and culture area from central/south Mexico through much of
central america, tapering out in Honduras and Nicaragua
- Great diversity of environments:
- Volcanoes of highland (central Mexico + Guatamala)
- Tropical Pacific + Atlantic coasts
- Rain Forests = Chiapas
- Suptropical flatlands = Yucatan
- Most people = lived in temperate climates
- Water + rich soils = permanent agriculture possible
- Lived in ethnic states ruled by hereditary nobilities
- Well-organized markets, marked social distinction, writing/recordkeeping system + calendar,
taxation, military, religions
- Produced: metal-work, fine textiles, ceramics, sculptures
- Although defined by Spaniards all as “indio”, identified with their local ethnic state (most never
traveled beyond it)
- Nahuas (Central Mexico)
- Dominant ethnic group in central Mexico
- Spoke Nahuatl language (still do so)
- Lived in thousands of ethnic states ( altepeme ) each consisting of capolli/tlaxilacalli
which paid tribute to dynastic ruler ( tlatoani )
- Most famous altepeme = Aztecs (called Mexico-Tenochtitlan), lived on island in Lake
Tetzcoco
- Refre to themselves as Mexica or Tenocha
- Mixteca Region (west of Oaxaca)
- In Mixteca region of Oaxaca, local state = nuu , subdivide into local siqui
- Bout 300,000 Mixtec speakers today
- Referred to by Nahuatl word mixteca (people of cloud place)
- Referred to themselves as taynudxahui (people of rain place) or people from
place of Dzahui (rain deity).
- M + F could rule nuu
- M = yya tonine
- F = yya dzehe tonine
- M + F ruler married → unite into yuhuitayu
- Yucatan (Maya)
- Ethnic state = cah
- Not divided into smaller parts
- Ruled by hereditary ruler ( Cahob )
- Aztec Empire
- Loose confederation of patchwork territories across central + southern Mexico
- Conquered between 1428 and 1521 by Triple Alliance
- Mexico-Tenochtitlan, Tetzcoco, and Tlacopan Altepl
- Plaid tribute + provided troops
- Colonial Period
- Mesoamerican diversity + persistence of local identity
- Historical experiences and ways common to Nahuas, Mixtecs, and Mayas
- Spanish Invasion
- 1st Spanish settlements in Caribbean → Cuba base of exploration
- 1519 Hernando Cortés bloody war against Mexica + allies (from Gulf Coast to
Mexico-Tenochtitlan)
- Outclassed militarily (steel, horses, gunpowder vs obsidian-blade, bow + arrows, spear)
- Diff tactics (capture vs kill)
- Aid of native allies
- Diseases (smallpox)
- 1521 (2 years of stiff resistance)
- Spaniards + allies defeat Mexica
- Relay fashion expand presence:
- Conquered place become source of spoils, funds, and supplies
- Historically no common identity, so little precedent for adopting common front
against enemy
- Representatives of Crown and Church arrived on scene to establish presence
- Tenochtitlan/Mexico City = greatest number b/c densely settled and wealthy
- Visibile presence of Crown (esp in Mexico City) but Spanish ruled indirectly
- Relied on local traditions + well-structured organization
- Municipal council = calbidos
- Mesoamerican communities represented by cabildo had access to colonia
legal system
- Recognized documents written by notaries in Mesoamerican languages
- Communities headed by native governor (descendent of local dynasty). Elected
local noblemen (never women) and alcaldes (judges) and regidores (councilmen)
- Friars + secular priests = “spiritual conquest”
- Parishes organized based on local communities
- Percentage of community’s labor + tribute towards church and priest
- Encomienda system
- Separate Spaniards and Mesoamericans in own communities (dissolve in
generation or 2) fastest around Mexico City
- Colonists grew → demand native labor → temporary labor grants, repartimiento .
- Move into cities → individual wage labor
- Mestizos (persons of mixed European/indigenous descent)
- Mulattoes (mixed African + European)
- Africans
- Spaniards
Chapter 2
- 1530s = teach alphabetic writing to indigenous elite
- 1540s - end of colonial = Mesoamericans produced great quantities of written records in own
languages using Roman alphabet
- 1st to adopt = Nahuas (central mexico), then Mixtecs, then Yucatec Mayas

5.3 Concerns Over the Sale of Nahua Noble’s Lands, Tlaxcala 1552
- Loss of lands by nobles who could no longer count on local commoners to work them
- Declining pop
- Spanish officials restricted number of people obligated to serve native lords (b/c also
competing for labor supply)
- Commoners also could get their own land more easily (b/c less people)
- → Lords would sell their lands to Spaniards
- → Decline of nobility (income depended on land and labor)
- “Many of the nobles who live as part of them have sold their fields and have been left not as
nobles but as commoners”
- Next 5-10 years lords’ houses’ fields are no more
- “Perhaps those who are in lordly houses will become ordinary commoners, and the commoners
who are buying the fields will be noblemen”
- Solution: no Tlaxcan noble can sell their fields
- If caught, relatives can take over fields or evict that person
